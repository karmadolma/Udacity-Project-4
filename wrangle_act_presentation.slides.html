WeRateDogs Tweet Data Wrangling Project
¶

For the purpose of this project, tweet archived data of Twitter user @dog_rates, also known as WeRateDogs was utilised. WeRateDogs is a Twitter account that rates people's dogs with a humorous comment about the dog. They have over 4 million followers. The project explored their basic tweet data (tweet ID, timestamp, text, etc.) for all 5000+ of their tweets as they stood on August 1, 2017.

What is Data Wrangling?
¶

Data wrangling is the process of gathering, selecting and transforming data to answer an analytical question
It is also known as data cleaning and also the step where 80% of the time is spent by analytics professional.(ref: www.elderresearch.com)

Gathering Data
¶

There were three data sources that was utilised: 1) First was the "Twitter Archive" data that had 5000+ of @WeRateDogs tweets that was provided already.

Assessing data

The goal of this steps is to identify any quality and tidiness issues. Assessment of data is done in two steps:
1) First is the visual assesssment where you eyeball the tables.
2) Second is the programmatic assessment where you use several functions such as info(), describer(), shape and so on.

A good principle is to document the issues identified as you go about assessing the data.

Quality
¶

Quality mainly refers to the content issues. Four of the main quality dimensions are:
1) Completeness: do we have all of the records that we should? Do we have missing records or not? Are there specific rows, columns, or cells missing?
2) Validity: Records that do not conform to the rules of data For example: negative height is impossible and also unique key constraints in tables).
3) Accuracy: Inaccurate data is wrong data that is valid. It adheres to the defined schema, but it is still incorrect. Example: a patient's weight that is 5 lbs too heavy because the scale was faulty.
4) Consistency: Consistency refers to the format of representing the data. For example: Newyork can be represented as "NEWYORK" or "NY".

Tidiness
¶

Tidiness mainly refers to the structural issues of the data.

For instance in the table below, Drug A, Drug B and Drug C can be transformed into two tables. One to specify the drug "Drug Type" and the second to specify the dosage "Dosage".
